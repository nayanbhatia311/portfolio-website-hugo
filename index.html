About Me

I am a Computer Science & Engineering PhD candidate at UC Santa Cruz, advised by Prof. Katia Obraczka in the Inter-Networking Research Group (i-NRG). My work turns commodity wireless telemetry into practical sensing systems, from LLM-based indoor localization to contactless vital-sign monitoring.

I develop and maintain the University of California's Essential Needs web app ecosystem, including the UC Essential Needs Directory and UC Essential Needs Navigator. UC Santa Cruz News reports that I developed the directory and AI-powered Navigator and implemented the complete technical system with campus partners. The platform serves a UC system that enrolled 301,093 students in fall 2025.

Research & Projects

  • WiFiGPT → Locaris / WiFi telemetry localization (2025–Present). WiFiGPT first framed CSI, RSSI, and FTM telemetry as a sequence-modeling problem for decoder-only transformers; Locaris extends that line into a compact, telemetry-agnostic localization model. The Locaris arXiv preprint reports sub-meter accuracy with a few hundred samples, robustness under missing APs, and telemetry-agnostic support; the UC technology-transfer record lists the related WiFiGPT disclosure as Tech ID 34231 / UC Case 2026-577-0, part of the pending patent process. WiFiGPT arXiv · Locaris arXiv · PerCom 2026 WiP announcement · UC Tech Transfer
  • Pulse-Fi (2024–Present). Low-cost, contactless WiFi CSI system for heart-rate monitoring, with extended work on breathing-rate and apnea monitoring. The peer-reviewed DCOSS-IoT paper reports heart-rate monitoring from low-cost WiFi devices; the extended arXiv version covers cardiopulmonary and apnea monitoring. Published at ACM Q2SWinet 2023, presented as an IEEE ICDCS 2025 tutorial, and supported by NSF POSE Award 2449377. ACM DOI · GitHub · ICDCS tutorial · NSF award
